Running with Proper Form

Runners spend a lot of time doing complex training routines, workouts, and such, However, many fail to grasp one of the more simple aspects of running: form. Good (or bad) form can make or break a tight race, and can seriously affect energy levels. The idea is to run in a manner that conserves the most energy. This involves not wasting energy in pointless ways. For example, people have a tendency to clench their fists while running, which is a useless waste of energy. A good way to measure this is to run while holding something fragile, like a potato chip. If you break the chip in your hands while running, you are clenching your fists too hard.
